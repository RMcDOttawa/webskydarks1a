Start and End Times

Dark and Bias frames can be acquired any time - after all, the camera shutter is closed. In fact, there is a school of thought that they should be acquired during daylight hours, so you are not wasting dark time when you could be acquiring real images. With a temperature-controlled camera this is certainly feasible. Without temperature control, darks should be acquired at about the same time of night as you will be imaging, so that the temperature-related dark noise is similar.

On the other hand, I personally like to take my dark frames at night, when it's cloudy. There are a few reasons for this:

  • First, it's often cloudy where I live, so I'm not wasting good imaging time.
  • Taking dark frames at night makes me more confident that no light is leaking through the camera shutter or housing.
  • Taking dark frames at night makes the camera cooling easier.

Whatever your reasons, if you want your dark frames to be taken during a specific time period, it can be convenient to automate the start and end, rather than having to be present to click "begin". The "Start & End" settings can control this

You can specify either or both of these properties:

  • Start Time: The session can start immediately, when you click Begin Session on the Run page, or it can start at some specified date and time in the future.

    In this latter case, when you click Begin Session, a delay timer starts. You must leave the WebSkyDarks program running for the timer to complete and acquisition to begin.

  • End Time: The session can run until it is finished, or it can run until a specified date and time in the future.

    In this latter case, the session will complete whatever image is in progress at the specified end time, then stop. The status of the acquisition is recorded, so you can pick it up on a future night and it will continue with whatever images still need to be acquired.
